About

MONARO

I was born into an ordinary family in Tashkent. There was no roadmap, no connections, no wealthy parents—only music, which always spoke to me louder than words.

From an early age, I was inspired by Nina Simone That's when I realized music is far more than sound. It has the power to change moods, unite people,and stay with us long after the last note fades. To me, music is the voice of the Earth

Six years ago, I stepped behind the DJ booth for the first time Since then, every set has been a search for that one moment when people stop thinking and simply begin to feel. I've built my own path—step by step, city by city. Today, The Earth is my home. But wherever I play, my purpose remains the same: to create an atmosphere where strangers become one

Vision

A set is not a playlist.
It's a place you take people.

Most DJs play tracks. MONARO builds moments. There's a difference between filling a room with sound and transforming it — and that difference is felt, not explained.

The approach is architectural. Every hour of music has a structure: tension, release, breath, climax. The crowd doesn't hear the architecture — they feel it in their chest.

Phnom Penh is the new chapter. A city with its own pulse, its own heat, its own way of moving after midnight. MONARO is here to be part of that story — and to write a new one.
